HISTORY

The first blood transfusion dates back to 16th century where a british physician RICHARD LOWER kept a dog alive in 1665 when he accidentally bled it to almost death and then revived the animal by tranfusing blood from another dog via a tied artery. The first human blood transfusion was done by JAMES BLUNDELL in 1818 to treat postpartum hemorrhage and was successfully executed in 1840 to treat hemophilia. In 1932 the first blood bank was established and they became famous in 1941 when CHARLES RICHARD DREW's research in the field of blood transfusions, improved techniques for blood transfusion.


WHAT IS BLOOD DONATION ?

A blood donation occurs when a person voluntarily has blood drawn and used for transfusions and/or made into biopharmaceutical medications by a process called fractionation. Donation may be of whole blood, or of specific components directly. Blood banks often participate in the collection process as well as the procedures that follow it.

WHY DONATE BLOOD ?

There is a constant need for a regular supply of blood because it can be stored only for a limited period of time before use. Regular blood donation by a sufficient number of healthy people is needed to ensure that blood will always be available whenever and wherever it is needed. HOW TO DONATE BLOOD ?

Blood donations in India are conducted by several organizations and hospitals by organizing blood donation camps. Donors can also visit blood banks in hospitals to donate blood or directly to a receiver. Several Registered Societies like Life Donors Kerala, Be Positive Blood Donation Group, Blood Donors Kerala, etc involve activity in blood donation activities around the globe. National Thalassemia Welfare Society established in 1991 for care and control of Thalassemia is organizing more than 60 camps every year and collecting over 4000 units per annum. Also this website maintains a database of blood donors which facilitates a network between blood donors and the recievers.
